Before Re:coded

I am from Iraq. I traveled to Syria in 2004 as a result of the American occupation of Iraq and the bad security conditions.
I graduated in the top ten of my class, from Aleppo University, Faculty of Management and Economics, Department of Business Administration.
I returned to Mosul, Iraq as a result of the Syrian crisis where I began a Master's program. Due to the entry of ISIS to Mosul, I was displaced to Erbil, in Northern Iraq where I finally completed my Master's of Business Administration, graduating with honors.

During Re:coded

I take every opportunity to learn and train, and strive to benefit from it.
I have entered many courses and I have a lot of training certificates. When I heard about this training course to learn programming and English, I told myself it was an opportunity to strengthen my language and learn something new.
It was one of the most difficult courses ever, because I did not have a background in programming, but it was a great opportunity.
The beauty of it is that I have met a lot of people from different cultural and educational backgrounds, of all age ranges.
And the concept of work has been developed, how to generate new ideas to start innovative projects and understand the labor market.
I have successfully completed the course with a lot of achievements and goals that I will pursue after this wonderful course.

After Re:coded

After completing the Re:coded course, I plan to continue learning programming to strengthen my level of knowledge. I will work teaching university and teaching programming languages and designing sites. There are a lot of plans and goals that I am thinking about because there is no ceiling for my ambitions.
